## Audio plan
A small audio set can sell the whole game.

- Idle hum loop.
- Paper feed sound.
- Jam alarm.
- Error beep.
- Fake reboot click.
- Victory chime.
- Failure buzz.

Even one or two strong audio cues will make the printer feel alive. In a UI game, sound does a lot of the emotional work.
## Art plan
You do not need complex art to make this work.

- One printer body illustration or icon set.
- Meter bars with color states.
- Simple warning flashes.
- Tiny job icons.
- Minimal office background.

A stylized dashboard look is enough. A few strong colors and readable typography will do more than detailed sprites.
## Content pipeline
The safest jam workflow is:
1. Implement systems.
2. Add placeholder text.
3. Fill event data in JSON or JS arrays.
4. Playtest and cut anything confusing.
5. Polish the text and pacing.

That order keeps the team from spending too much time on assets before the game is fun.
